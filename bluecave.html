Biševo Island, once known for its vineyards and olives, today best known for Blue Cave thanks to the thousands tourist that visit this natural phenomenon every year. The Cave became famous in 1884. when Baron Eugen von Ransonnet suggested opening an artificial entrance large enough for small boats to enter. Originally the Cave has one entrance under the sea level and was accessible only by diving. For that very entrance the Cave became so famous. It is through this submarine­like opening on the cave that sunlight gets in and creates aquamarine blue glowing effect all around the cave. The light reflects through the water coming from the white floor of the cave and bathes the cave in an amazing aquamarine light while objects in the water appear to be silver. But there are no words that could describe this amazing beauty, it is something that you should experience yourself.
