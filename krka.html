Krka Waterfalls
Krka Waterfalls
  • Duration: 09:00h - 17:00h
  • Price per person: 450kn (60€)
  • Price includes: transfer, entrance fees for sights, english speaking guide, Short walking tour in Šibenik and Trogir, Possibility of half daychoosing duration of tour / half day or day trip, Insurance
  • Itinerary:
    09:00: departure from Sun and Sea T.A.
    10:00: arrival to Krka Waterfalls
    14:30: departure from Skradin
    15:00: short stop and walking tour in Šibenik and Trogir
    17:00: arrival to Split
National park Krka got its name after Krka river that springs from the mountain Dinara foot. It stretches in length of 73 km with seven travertine waterfalls of exceptional beauty. The highest waterfall is called Roški, 32 m high, you can enjoy its beauty in one of the many restaurants surrounding it. After Roški, Krka continues its flow to Skradinski buk waterfall, the most impressive waterfall. Before experiencing the beauty of Skradinski buk, turn your route to the Visovac island, small island in the middle of the homonymous Visovac lake. Visit the island monastery that keeps numerous items from the Roman empire time. Franciscans, that take care of the monastery museum, grow various herbs so today there are 195 different plant species. Now prepare to be breath taken the first time you see Skradinski buk, the most impressive waterfall consisted of 17 travertine cascades. With the waterfall, nearby watermill, rolling mills the creates outstanding natural and cultural heritage.

Preserving the traditional folk architecture, individual mills have been turned into souvenir shops, while others have been transformed into exhibition areas for ethnographic collections. Krka meets the see in Skradin, a pretty, romantic little riverside town with a combination of brightly painted and bare stone houses on its main street and a ruined fortress towering above. Apart from the opportunity to see the town itself, characterised by a true Dalmatian spirit with authentic taverns that serve only traditional specialities, the advantage of starting in Skradin is that the park admission includes a boat ride through the canyon to Skradinski Buk.

You will have opportunity to learn more about this amazing national park from local tour guide and later on discover this pearl of Dalmatia by yourself – by walking on walking trails or swimming in front of Skradinski buk. Skradinski buk is one of most beautiful waterfalls in Krka National Park and this area is prefect for enjoying nature. In addition to that, you will have the opportunity to see historical towns Trogir and Šibenik on our short walking tour on the way back.
